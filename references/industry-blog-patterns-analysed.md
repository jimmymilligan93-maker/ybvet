# Benchmark blog patterns (analysed) — style reference only

This document distils **recurring patterns** from a **private Firecrawl** of a **24/7 emergency-style veterinary education blog index** operated elsewhere in the **same parent ownership** as YB Vet. It exists so writers and agents can match **tone, structure, and pacing** — as if the **same human** could have drafted YB Vet posts.

**Hard rules for `ybvet.com.au`:**

- **Do not** name that sister business.  
- **Do not** link to it.  
- **Do not** import its phone numbers, addresses, hours, or on-site capabilities.  
- It is **not** a transcript: do **not** paste long passages from the crawl into published copy.

**YB Vet** is a **stand-alone day + Saturday morning** practice at **Yangebup** unless `lib/data.ts` says otherwise.

---

## 1. Topic clusters (categories only — rewrite for YB Vet scope)

Themes useful for **educational** posts (titles and angles in your own words):

- Toxins & ingestions; environmental / WA heat; trauma / choking / fractures; seizures; bloat awareness; seasonal hazards; “what to expect at a consult” style reassurance.

Prioritise what **YB Vet** actually delivers; use **generic** emergency routing language when topics touch after-hours crises.

---

## 2. Structural patterns (safe to emulate)

- Scene-first cold open → short paragraphs → frequent `##` subheads.  
- Bullets for symptoms, timing, what to bring, what **not** to DIY.  
- Explicit “when to book here” vs “when this is an emergency.”  
- Closing CTA: **only** YB Vet phone + Vetstoria from `lib/data.ts` unless leadership approves more.

---

## 3. Tone and rhythm (abstracted)

- Second person; species-aware; calm authority; acknowledges stress.  
- Plain Australian English; avoid corporate wellness clichés (see `voice.md` banned list).  
- Educational flow: mechanism → what to watch → what happens in a **day practice** consult — without inventing equipment.
