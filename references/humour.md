# Humour — YB Vet

## Default: light touch, optional

Most pages do not need jokes. **Clarity and kindness** beat wit — especially when an owner is worried about a sick pet.

**Benchmark alignment (style only):** The parent group’s **benchmark blog set** (from a private crawl used for patterns) uses humour **rarely** — mostly **dry, practical** asides (e.g. small friction in getting a pet through the door). YB Vet should feel like the **same author could have written here**: same restraint, same instinct to cut a joke if it doesn’t help. **Do not** name or link the sister property in published copy.

When humour fits (e.g. a light seasonal note in a blog):

- Keep it **gentle and dry**, never at the **pet’s** or **owner’s** expense.
- Avoid clinical topics as punchlines (pain, euthanasia, serious diagnoses, obesity, aggressive behaviour).
- No edgy, political, or divisive humour in clinic-owned content.

## What does not work here

- Meme-speak, forced “relatable” quirkiness, or puns on suffering.
- Self-deprecation that undermines trust (“we never answer the phone” — even as a joke).

## Safe patterns

- **Observational:** Perth heat, school holidays, “carrier season” for cats — as **context**, not the main point.
- **Warm asides** tied to approved stories in `stories.md`, told straight — not as a gag.

## Rule of thumb

If removing the joke makes the page **stronger**, remove it. If a line might worry someone whose pet is unwell, cut it.
