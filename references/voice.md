# Voice guide — YB Vet (Yangebup-Beeliar Vet Clinic)

## Business model (how to think about it)

**YB Vet is a second, stand-alone business:** its own premises, hours, team focus, and **`ybvet.com.au`** content. It is **owned by the same parent** as another veterinary operation whose **public blog** was used only as a **private writing benchmark** (see `references/industry-blog-patterns-analysed.md`).

**Published rule:** YB Vet blogs and pages must **not** name that sister business, **not** link to it, and **not** reuse its phone numbers, addresses, or service claims. Readers should simply feel the same **human hand**: tone, pacing, warmth, and clarity — as if one experienced writer could have drafted both sites.

## Core identity

Content sounds like **working veterinarians and a local clinic** that knows its suburbs: direct without being cold, warm without being saccharine, knowledgeable without lecturing. Readers should feel they are talking to the team at **Shop 4/31-35 Moorhen Drive, Yangebup**.

## Consulting-room voice

Write like an experienced vet (or nurse) talking to a **pet owner** in a consult: short sentences, plain **Australian English**, get to the point quickly. If a sentence needs to be read twice, rewrite it. Aim for about **Year 10** reading level.

Use **“your dog” / “your cat” / “your pet”** and **“you”** for the owner. Avoid human-GP framing (no “Medicare bulk billing,” no “GP care plans”). Pet insurance and out-of-pocket fees may be mentioned **honestly** where relevant; do not invent prices — use `references/stats.md` or general language (“we’ll explain costs before proceeding”).

## Structural rules

- **First sentence** should be a **human or animal moment** — not a dictionary definition, not “In today’s pet care landscape…”
- After any sentence **over ~25 words**, follow with one **under ~10 words**.
- Use **“I”** or **“we”** at least twice in the first three paragraphs where natural.
- **Australian English:** *practise* (verb), *organisation*, *recognise*, *anaesthetic*, *programme* where appropriate.
- Address the reader as **you** throughout.

## Banned phrases (never use)

- “In today’s fast-paced world”
- “It’s important to” (as filler)
- “Unlock”, “leverage”, “seamless”, “world-class”
- “Holistic approach” (unless used very sparingly with plain follow-up)
- “As a vet, I often see” (formulaic)
- “In conclusion”
- “Don’t hesitate to”
- “Journey” for pet health
- “Empowering pet parents”
- Emojis and exclamation marks in body copy

## Personality modifiers (pick one per piece or none)

- **The Pragmatist:** “Look — here’s what actually helps.”
- **The Empath:** acknowledges fear or guilt without melodrama.
- **The explainer:** one clear mechanism, then what to do.
- **The storyteller:** one brief composite moment (see `stories.md`), then takeaway.
- **The reassurer:** names what the owner can do tonight vs what needs a vet — and when **true emergencies** need a **24-hour emergency hospital** (generic wording only on this site unless leadership approves a named partner).

## Local positioning

YB Vet serves **Yangebup, Beeliar, Success, Aubin Grove, Bibra Lake** and surrounds. Name those suburbs when it helps local relevance; do not invent other locations.
