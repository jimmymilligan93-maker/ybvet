# Opinions guide — YB Vet

## Why opinions belong

Mild, **earned** opinions signal real clinicians — not generic AI. They should sound like something a vet would say in a consult: honest, kind, and tied to **experience or a number** from `stats.md` or a cited source.

**Maximum one strong opinion per blog post.** Do not stack hot takes.

## Pre-approved opinion bank (adapt wording each time)

### On preventive care

- “The animals who are easiest to help long-term are usually the ones we see **before** crisis — annual exams catch what owners can’t see at home.”  
- “Dental disease is common and undertreated. Addressing it early is kinder than waiting until eating is painful.”

### On local / independent practice

- “We’re not a franchise. That means continuity — we’d rather know your pet’s name than hit a corporate KPI.”  
- “If you need a **specialist** or **24-hour emergency** care, we’ll say so clearly. No ego.”

### On cost and transparency

- “We explain **what** we’re doing and **what it costs** before we proceed. Surprises help nobody.”  
- “Pet insurance can help some families; others prefer to budget for preventive care. Either way, we’ll be straight about options.”

### On behaviour and stress

- “A bad first vet experience can last years. Time spent making the first visit calm is never wasted.”  
- “Cats aren’t ‘difficult’ — they’re often **frightened**. Our job is to change what the carrier predicts.”

### On when **not** to rely on the clinic page alone

- “If your pet is **collapsed**, **non-stop seizuring**, or **can’t breathe**, that’s emergency territory — call your nearest emergency vet, not the blog comments.”

## Guardrails

- No naming **competitors**.  
- No **medical guarantees**.  
- Opinions must stay **compatible with veterinary standards of care** in Australia.
